Portfolio

Lighting

Iphigenia: Dead/Alive

World premiere production at Abington Friends School (AFS). A brand new, partially devised adaptation of Iphigenia at Aulis and Iphigenia among the Taurians. Directed by Kittson O'Niell and the AFS directing team, scenic design by Jo Zemsky. Photos by John Flak.

Served as lead designer, collaborating with Oliver Peterson. Additionally served as electrician and programmer.

Built practicals, drafted and hung the plot, and built atmospheric effects.

Beauty and the Beast Jr.

AFS Middle School production, directed by Mary Carpentar. Photos by John Flak.

Served as lead designer with Oliver Peterson.

Worked from preexisting AFS Muller Auditorium Rep plot. Mentored Middle School technicains including calling SM, spotlight ops and board op.

Urinetown

Music by Mark Holloman, Book by Greg Kotis, Lyrics by Mark Holloman and Greg Kotis, Directed and Choreographed by Doug Weyand, Music Directed by Matt Marco. Lighting design by Mallory Tirone

Worked with Mallory Tirone as Programmer and Board Op, and assisted with light hang as UB department electrician. This production provided excellent learning opportunities such as pixel mapping with EOS and hightened effects requirements. Solely served as Programmer rather than designer as well, emulating professional practice.

AFS Muller Rentals

From 2023-2025, worked as house LD and programmer on rentals of the AFS Muller Auditorium space, ranging from dance schools to Rabbinical College graduations. Also served occasionally as part of run and fly crew.

Scenic

Urinetown

A second (different) production of Urinetown performed at the AFS Muller Auditorium. Directed by Kittson O'Niell, music direction by Justin Solonynka,

As part of Technical Theater Design, taught by Seth Schmitt-Hall, worked with small team to collaborativly design the set. Collaborated with Jo Zemsky to develop concept art of cityscape, then created model and technical drawings on Vectorworks and built the flat. Led wiring of LED tape for the city and underlighting of the platform with Tamara Brockington. Also conceptualized and executed falling effect for Bobby's Death (who is thrown off a building during the course of the show), drawing inspiration from John Napier's original design of Les Miserables.
